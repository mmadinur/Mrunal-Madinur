Inside 9 San Francisco VC Firms

This past week I had the fortunate opportunity to visit 9 venture capital firms in San Francisco: Pear VC, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Alumni Ventures, Unusual Ventures, Amino Capital, Entrepreneurs First, Costanoa Ventures, Andreessen Horowitz, and Altos Ventures.

Below are my core takeaways.

VC Strategies

As most know, no investment firm's strategy is 100% identical. However, it is often difficult to differentiate between mandates while solely utilizing public information.

To start off with the commonalities. Virtually all of these VCs are technology oriented investors with a follow-on focused investment strategy regardless of their initial entry stage.

Quite literally the earliest stage in the entire ecosystem is Entrepreneurs First, a firm that invests pre-idea. The firm aims to invest in driven technical founders prior to any formation of ideas. They provide initial equity free funding, over $600K in credits, housing, and operational support to scale ideas.

Pear VC takes a standard early stage investing approach backing strong founders with ideas. Investors primarily make pre-seed bets and offer operational expertise. They utilize their PearX accelerator to identify exciting opportunities to back in the future.

Unusual Ventures and Costanoa Ventures have similar mandates. Both specialize in concentrated seed stage bets with a hands on operator approach. The team consists of numerous operators that specialize in sales, go-to-market, product market fit, and more.

Amino Capital, a firm that also specializes in seed stage bets, takes a more founder friendly approach. Investors do not actively offer their expertise unless approached.

Moving forward, Altos Ventures focuses on concentrated Series A bets backing capital efficient businesses. The firm makes 8–10 investments a year, roughly one investment a year for each partner, and leaves the remaining time for deep operational help.

Lastly, Alumni Ventures, Lightspeed Venture Partners, and Andreessen Horowitz utilize a spray and pray approach. Investors make hundreds of investments a year across all stages. The goal is to allocate their capital across a multitude of companies in order to gain access into follow-on rounds for their most successful companies.

Consensus vs. Non-Consensus Bets

Barry Eggers, the founder of Lightspeed Venture Partners, mentioned that VC has shifted from diversified portfolios to concentrating capital into winning companies.

All VCs agreed that those winning companies exist within non-consensus bets.

Venture Investing Within a Rapidly Shifting Environment

How do VCs gain strong conviction in an ecosystem that is rapidly innovating?

Niamh O'Donnell at Unusual Ventures referred to Jeff Bezos' opinion. Both believe that the true answer to this question is to think not about what will change in 10 years but rather what stones will remain unturned in that period.

By utilizing this philosophy you are able to back track into an effective thesis about the future market landscape.

Hedgehog vs. Fox

"The fox knows many things; the hedgehog knows one big thing." This is originally a quote from 7th-century BCE Greek poet Archilochus, and it now drives Altos Ventures' investment mandate.

Like many, I am a victim of being deeply curious in attaining surface level knowledge about everything. I find myself skipping from new topic to new topic, and struggling to get through subjects due to excitement in other concepts.

However, the world's most successful people are ones that are deeply specialized in specific subject matter.

Sean Cai, a 23 year old venture investor at Costanoa Ventures, is an example. He is not overly skilled in any operational expertise, or a huge visionary, rather he is deeply experienced and educated in infrastructure AI.

Similar to this, venture investors want to back founders who firmly understand a certain market and are attacking truly specific pain points. This solidifies that founders will not jump ship, and that they are dedicated to their core thesis.
